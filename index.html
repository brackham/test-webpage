My Research

Finding new worlds

Cool stars with transiting exoplanets provide our best chance to study small, temperate exoplanets in the next 20 years.

Characterizing their host stars

To understand these exoplanets, we first must understand their host stars.

Studying their atmospheres

With this foundation, we can effectively study the atmospheres of these worlds with transit spectroscopy.

Outreach

I am committed to furthering the impact of my research through teaching, mentoring, and public outreach efforts. Most of the efforts I've been involved in are listed in my CV. Some recent highlights are detailed below.

Project POEM

Project POEM aims to foster interest in STEM careers among visually impaired middle and high school students. As part of the kick-off week for Project POEM at the UA SkyCenter atop Mt. Lemmon in 2018, I developed and led an activity that encouraged 12 participants to explore the properties of transiting exoplanets using sonified transit light curves. I revised the activity in 2019 and delivered it to 14 Project POEM participants.

The activity materials are available on GitHub.

Teen Astronomy Café

The Teen Astronomy Café Program brings cutting-edge astronomical research to high school students in the Tucson area. Professional astronomers develop and deliver engaging actvities that introduce the big questions in their field and the tools we use to address them. Along with Dániel Apai, I developed and led an activity that explored how we discover and characterize exoplanets with the transit technique. The activity uses Python and Jupyter Notebook to familiarize students with tools that astronomers use for this research.

The activity materials are available on GitHub.
